Legend 6: A Strange Wind

by Jameson Meyer

[ source | more legends ]

The Queen and Prince Thorald have declared a time of celebration; great levity can be found throughout the whole of the kingdom. The masses were saved from the terror of the Mighty Dragon and the kingdom was again at peace and free from the plottings of the Dark Mage Varkur. The Heroes were enjoying some well-deserved time off at the local tavern, as the Prince himself led them in drinking games and endless rounds of songs.

It was barely dawn and the sun was just about to peak over the horizon, the heroes were just waking from their drunken slumbers and approaching the innkeeper to order their first round of the day when an unnatural wind threw open the door and pushed through the inn, sending a chill down the spine of everyone inside. When it finally died down, the heroes swore they could hear a voice calling to them, saying “Darkness is alive, inside...”

Just as the heroes were going to comment about the strangely familiar disembodied voice, a young man came running through the doorway. “Heroes come quickly! There is a battlement forming at the tree of songs!”

“That wind was trying to tell us something..” said the first hero pensively.

“Don’t be ridiculous,” said the other “The air cannot speak, come! To the forest, and to Glory!”

The heroes again revel in their amazing battle prowess and joy in their accomplishments. Just as they take a long sigh of relief the same cold wind as before rushes over the hillside and sinks into their bones. On the wind, the same familiar voice can be heard, whispering “Darkness is alive, inside…” The heroes shake themselves.

“That voice...it’s so familiar, who could it be?” a hero wonders aloud.

A smile creeps onto the other heroes face “Aye, let’s see, who do we know that deals in all things creepy and magical, and is obsessed with darkness? It’s Varkur! The bloody Dark Mage is back!” he shouts, and draws his weapon, ready to attack the wind.

“No…”the other hero whispers. “It’s not a threat I think it’s a warning. But who would warn us about dark magic?” The hero doesn’t finish, understanding floods through him, and without a word, he begins to run toward the west.

“Where are you going?!” the other hero shouts.

“Come on!” he beckons. “We have to find the Witch!”

The heroes again revel in their amazing battle prowess and joy in their accomplishments. Just as they are climbing down from the tower, a soldier appears and tells them that he has been to the farm and found the farmer and his family dead.

When the heroes reach the witches home, they find it in shambles. They make their way through the small cottage and find furniture overturned and countless bodies of soldiers again bearing the sigil of Reitburg Castle guards.

“There was a fight here,” one hero said. “But what are the guards doing so far from the castle?”

They followed the signs of struggle to the surrounding woods, scorch marks tore into the trees and bodies littered the ground; the smell of burnt wood and flesh hung thickly in the air. The battle seemed to have culminated at a ring of charred earth and timber that formed a circle; five strides wide, around a single body. As they moved closer they recognize it as the prince.

Moving him onto his back, the heroes discover that he is still breathing. He opens his eyes slowly, and looks up at them.

“...too late,” he muttered. “...too late,” the prince moans again before falling unconscious.

“First the wind speaks, then the Gor organize themselves, Castle guards are killed by other soldiers in the borderlands, now this?!” a hero exclaims.

“Yes,” says another, “something is definitely amiss in the castle.” The hero pulls out the letter they had been carrying. “I think it might be time to read this.”

“Are you mad?!” replies the other. “Only royalty can break a royal seal?! Or it’s treason!”

“I guess we have to find a royal, then…”

Place the Prince Thorald marker on its side in the location of the Witch fog token. While the prince is wounded, the heroes do not receive a bonus to their attack.

The heroes must decide if they want to:

  • Take the letter and wounded prince to the queen. All heroes and the prince must venture to the Reitburg Castle (space 0). The prince can only move 2 spaces at a time instead of 4 as he is wounded, and he does not give any bonus while in combat.
  • Or wait here for an entire day for the prince to recover and read the letter.

The heroes are greeted as they enter the castle grounds and are escorted to the Queen. Upon entering the ornately decorated throne room, the Queen is shocked to see the prince is alive and gives orders for him to be taken to his chambers and heavily guarded.

The heroes give her the letter they found.(Remove scroll from inventory and return it to the game box)

The Queen opens the letter and sighs audibly. The heroes look at each other in confusion. “What’s wrong, your majesty?” one hero asks.

“It appears that our plea for help didn’t make it,” she says.

“A plea for help? To whom? Is this about the voice we’ve been hearing?” they ask.

“It is, but I doubt you will hear it any longer. It appears that the prince has been in league with the Dark Mage Varkur, as his apprentice. He has been using his new powers to alter the mind of the witch Reka to doing his bidding.”

The heroes gasp in surprise. “But her warning?”

“Yes, Reka is very strong” the Queen continued, “She was able to fight against the prince’s powers to try and warn you about him.”

“Darkness is alive, inside…” a hero whispers. “She meant inside the castle?”

The Queen nodded. “I’m afraid so. We were writing to the keepers of the Tree of Songs to see if they could use their healing magic to help her, but the prince’s spies must have found our messenger.”

The doors to the throne room burst open and a frantic soldier runs up to the queen, falling to his knees. “Your majesty! The prince has died from his wounds and the witch is gone!”

The building rumbles as a scream rings through the castle and echoes off the stone walls.

A hero drew his weapon, “You have the witch here?!” he shouted.

“Yes,” the Queen exclaimed worriedly, “I heard her warning, too and sent a regiment of my personal guards to escort her here so she wouldn’t have to hide in the woods any longer. They told me that the prince fought them but was eventually killed, so they brought her here until she was well again.”

Screams shook the building so violently that the glass windows behind the throne shattered.

“But if he is dead, then why is Reka doing this?!” A hero exclaimed.

A bolt of lightning shot through the broken windows and into the throne room, sending the heroes and the Queen flying in all directions. As the heroes looked to the center of the room, where the bolt had hit, they saw the witch, she was bathed in purple shadow; her eyes glowing with a fiendish blue hue. “Because,” she shouted “I, Thorald, have taken over the witch’s mind completely, and now, the kingdom will be mine!”
